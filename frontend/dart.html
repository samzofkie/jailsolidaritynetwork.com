Sheriff Office's Narrative v. Reality

Sources for quotes from Sheriff Office and Cook County Jail officials linked below.

All 2023 testimony was collected from insiders by the Jail Solidarity Network.

about mental health treatment

In 2017, Dart asserted, "95 percent of the people in this jail are waiting on a trial. So everybody here are people who haven't been convicted yet. So you say to yourself, 'All right, they're presumed innocent. Who is so dangerous that we need to hold them here while we're waiting on a trial?'

 

[...] If they're going to make it so that I am going to be the largest mental health provider, we're going to be the best ones. We're going to treat 'em as a patient while they're here, it's like, we are going to think differently."

​

Read the full story here.

Sheriff Dart agreed that many people inside deserve to be released and receive treatment...

...yet claims people need to be imprisoned longer, and redirects taxpayer money to the jail. 

During October 2023 budget hearings, Sheriff Dart "complained that the end of cash bail [through the Pretrial Fairness Act] had reduced the time for corrections employees to work with detainees addicted to opioids."

​

He said: “All the treatment is hindered by the fact a lot of these folks are just with me for this little window of time."

Meanwhile, people living in the jail say they only receive treatment when they get out or go to prison, if ever.

about medical care

"Two people were having seizures back to back. They fell off the top bunk, bonked their heads, big old knots on the head. I'm on Klonopin. I was supposed to be given those, was never given those. I'm shaking now. I've been on those since like second grade. Pain also, I've been shot so I've got nerve damage and everything and was never nothing for that neither. Was not taken care of. I was treated like a regular inmate and just thrown in there [...] Most COs were supposed to check in on us, only one person asked me if I was okay. Everyone else just flashed the lights on and off. I asked for ibuprofen and they said no. I have nerve damage. I was beaten by a cop. And they said no."
